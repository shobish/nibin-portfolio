Accelerating Progress:
Meet Nibin Mathew

For Nibin Mathew, accessibility is more than just a mission—it’s a driving force behind everything he does. As a seasoned technologist and accessibility specialist, Nibin harnesses the power of technology to create social change, breaking down barriers and confronting ableism head-on. His work is shaping a more inclusive world, where technology serves all, regardless of ability, and no one is left behind.

His passion for sport is equally remarkable. Nibin became the first Indian blind tennis player to win a medal at the IBSA World Games 2023 in London, securing his place in history at the International Tennis Championship. His journey, both in the digital world and on the tennis court, reflects his conviction that meaningful change begins with small, determined actions, paving the way for a brighter, more inclusive future.

Driving Action, Creating Impact

Global Accessibility Awareness Day 2024

Watch as Nibin reflects on the role of assistive technology in his everyday life, enabling him to live with independence and dignity. He calls on us to rethink how we view disability and society, and advocates for the use of technology as a powerful tool for driving social progress.

Keep the Momentum: National Blind Tennis Champion 2024

Nibin continues his journey by retaining his title as the national blind tennis champion following his bronze medal at the IBSA World Games 2023. He secured his second consecutive national medal, being honored at the Blind Tennis Championship held in Goa during the International Purple Fest 2024.

State Award for Best Sportsperson 2024

In recognition of his international victory, the Government of Kerala honored Nibin with the 2024 Person with Disability Award, celebrating his exceptional achievements as a sportsperson in the state.

India’s First Blind Tennis Medalist at the IBSA World Games, London 2023

Nibin etched his name in history as the first Indian to compete in blind tennis on the global stage. Representing India at the prestigious IBSA World Games 2023 in Birmingham, London, Nibin secured a bronze medal. His achievement not only marked a personal triumph but also a proud moment for India, as he became the first Indian to raise the national flag in blind tennis at this renowned international event.
